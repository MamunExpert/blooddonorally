Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about blood donation, donor privacy, emergency support, donor verification and platform usage.

Our donor directory allows you to search donors by blood group, district, city and availability status. During an emergency, you can use the search filters to quickly locate suitable donors near your location. Once you find a donor, you may contact them directly through phone, Messenger or WhatsApp depending on the donor's privacy settings.
Yes. Donor privacy is one of our highest priorities. Donors may choose to hide or protect their phone numbers from public view. In those cases, alternative communication methods such as Messenger will remain available. This helps protect donors from spam calls, unwanted contact and misuse of personal information.
A verified donor is a donor whose profile has been manually reviewed by our volunteer team. Verification helps increase trust and confidence within the donor network. While verification does not guarantee donor availability at all times, it helps users identify authentic and active donor profiles more easily.
Most healthy adults can become blood donors. Generally, donors should meet minimum age and health requirements defined by medical authorities. Individuals with certain medical conditions or recent illnesses may need temporary or permanent restrictions. Always consult healthcare professionals if you are unsure about your eligibility.
In emergency situations, every minute can be critical. We encourage donors to respond as quickly as possible whenever they are available and medically fit to donate. Even if you are unable to donate yourself, sharing the request within your network can help save valuable time.
Yes. Blood donation is generally considered very safe for healthy individuals. Donation procedures follow strict medical standards and sterile equipment is used for every donor. Most donors return to their normal routine shortly after donating blood.
First, search for matching blood group donors using the donor directory. Contact multiple donors simultaneously to improve response speed. Always provide accurate patient details, hospital information and urgency level so donors can make informed decisions quickly.
Yes. We respect the privacy of every donor and volunteer. Privacy features such as protected phone numbers, profile controls and secure data management help ensure that personal information remains protected.
Blood compatibility is extremely important during transfusion. O− can donate to all blood groups and is known as the Universal Donor. AB+ can receive blood from all groups and is known as the Universal Recipient. General compatibility: • O− → Everyone • O+ → O+, A+, B+, AB+ • A− → A−, A+, AB−, AB+ • A+ → A+, AB+ • B− → B−, B+, AB−, AB+ • B+ → B+, AB+ • AB− → AB−, AB+ • AB+ → AB+ only Always follow hospital and physician recommendations before any transfusion.
